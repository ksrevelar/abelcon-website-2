What is Abelcrete®?

A wall system that sprays concrete with high velocity using a wet mixture composed of cement, sand, and water onto an installed rebars with mesh.

System Evaluation

Advantages & Disadvantages

Advantages

Expedited Construction

As compared to traditional CHB lay-out, Abelcrete® Wall System is computed to be 40% faster. Idle time in this system is lesser. No need for plastering, and no need for chipping.

High Versatility & Design Flexibility

Extremely adaptable and useful for a variety of applications, such as high walls, domes, and other architecturally curved finished surfaces.

Superb Strength

The system uses a pressurized nozzle to spray mortar which leads to a high strength, low permeability and high durability walls.

Fire Resistant

The Abelcrete Wall System can withstand up to 4 hrs of direct exposure and is not flammable up to 980°C.

Excellent Sound Absorption

The system has a sound transmission class of minimum 42 dB and maximum 47 dB, reducing or eliminating loud noises and disturbances. Ideal for structures that require acoustic insulation.

Disadvantages

Skilled and Experienced Manpower

The installation of this wall system needs highly skilled laborers.

Specialized Equipment

The Abelcrete® Wall System requires specific equipment such as a mini-avant turbosol as a concrete sprayer.

Work Productivity Varies on MEPFS Works

The walling system requires the pre-requisite works (M.E.P.F.S Works) to finish the installation of roughing ins before application of scratch coat to optimize and obtain the productivity of the walling system.

Requires Wider Area to be more Efficient

The system requires a wider area for the preparation to be more effective and efficient as compared to the traditional wall systems.

Installation Methodology

1

Area Preparation

Proper clearing of debris for both slabs, columns, and beam soffits to ensure that there are no loose concrete.

2

Lay-Out Wall Partition

Layout wall partition based on approved plans. Coordinate the partition layout to ensure that the MEPFS roughing ins will not be exposed.

3

Installation of Dowels

For Steel Structure: Install dowels via welding machine. For Concrete Structure: Install dowels using electric or battery-operated drill.

4

Installation of Rebars

Install both horizontal and vertical rebars and add additional C-bar as an additional reinforcement for both stiffener columns and lintel beams.

5

Installation of Plastic Mesh

Install plastic mesh flat on rebars and tie it together using tie wire and cutting nipper.

6

Installation of MEPFS Works

Install M.E.P.F.S. roughing ins and ensure that all the pipes are within the desired thickness.

7

Application of Scratch Coat

Apply scratch coat (mixture of cement & sand) on the surface of the plastic mesh. This will serve as the stopper once concrete spraying starts.

8

Application: Part 1 - Thickening

This will serve as the first coat of Abelcrete®. At least half of the wall thickness must be applied as the thickener.

9

Application: Part 2 - Establishing

The second coat establishes the wall thickness by constructing "Pilete". This serves as the guide in screeding the wall.

10

Application: Part 3 - Filling

The last step in spraying. This ensures there will be no shallow part on the wall partitions. With the help of "pilete" and continuous screeding, a straight finish is achieved.

11

Finishing of Wall Partition

By utilizing the "pakatas" technique of our skilled masons and a hand trowel made of stainless steel, there will be a very smooth wall partition finish.

12

Housekeeping

To ensure that the area will be free of any loose concrete and laitance, housekeeping is an essential part of the installation procedure.
